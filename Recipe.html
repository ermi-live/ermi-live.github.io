Habesha's Recipe Book

Travel to Sicily

Habesa refers to both Eritrean and Ethiopian people. Eritrean cuisine shares similarities with surrounding countries' cuisines; however, the cuisine has its unique characteristics.

The main traditional food in Eritrean cuisine is tsebhi (stew), served with injera (flatbread made from teff, wheat, or sorghum) and hilbet (paste made from legumes; mainly lentil and faba beans). A typical traditional Eritrean dish consists of injera accompanied by a spicy stew, which frequently includes beef, goat, lamb or fish. Overall, Eritrean cuisine strongly resembles that of neighboring Ethiopia, although Eritrean cooking tends to feature more seafood than Ethiopian cuisine on account of its coastal location. Eritrean dishes are also frequently lighter in texture than Ethiopian meals as they tend to employ less seasoned butter and spices and more tomatoes, as in tsebhi derho.

1.Qulwa

Top, Tsebhi Derho, Tsebhi Shiro

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 6 persons

Ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Beef 1 kg
Onion 1 piece
Oil 1 tablespoon
Fresh Tomato 1 large piece
Oregano & Thym 1 teaspoon
Zucchini 1 piece

Preparation Steps

  1. Prepare a bigger pan and place it on a swithched on stove
  2. Pour 1 table spoon of oil on to the pan
  3. When the oil on the pan is heated ,then add the beef (minced into 1 inch size) and stir it untill all the beef turned into greyish color.
  4. Then add the diced onion (1 cm size) and diced zucchini (1 inch size) in to the pan and continue stiring to turn over the uncooked sides of the added ingredients.
  5. When the added onion and zucchini are also cooked, then add diced tomato(1 inch size) and the oregano spice in to the cooking pan and continue stiring to mix the added ingredients.
  6. Finally taste the tenderness of the beef and if it is good upto your likes then add a pinch of salt for extra taste and take it off the cooking stove and serve it on top of Injera laid on a plate.

2.Tsebhi Derho

Top, Qulwa, Tsebhi Shiro

  • Time: 90 minutes
  • Serves: 12 persons

Ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Whole Chicken 1 piece
Onion 1 kg
Oil 2 tablespoon
Tomato Paste 400 g
Mixed spices 1 tablespoon
Habesha Peper powder/Berbere 2 tablespoon
Habesha Butter/Tesmi 2 tablespoon
Egg 12 pieces

Preparation Steps

  1. Prepare a bigger pan and place it on a swithched on stove
  2. Add diced onion (1/2 cm size) into the pan and keep stiring it so that the onion will be cooked in all sides with out getting burnedand.when the onion color turned into grey-yellowish then add the oil in to the pan and cook it unitl the onion turned into golden color.
  3. When the oil and onion are cooked,then add the habeha peper poweder/Berbere and keep stiring it untill all the added peper are mixed and become uniform with the ingredients. At this point taste the mix and the added peper should be of good taste and smell not strong spicy and irritating smell.
  4. Then add the tomato paste to the pan and stir it ot mixed well with the already cooked ingredients and cook it untill the smell and taste of the tomato paste disappear.
  5. When the taste of tomato paste disappears , then added all the chicken parts (the chicken should be washed and cut in to body part pieces before you put it into the cooking pan) and stir it to mix it well with the rest in the pan. Continue stiring the cooking pan so that all the ingredients mixa and cooked well.
  6. After a while add the mixed spices of Oregano, Blackpeper and Thym in to the cooking pan and continue stiring to mix the added ingredients.
  7. After cooking for 1 hour then taste the tenderness of the chicken and if it is good upto your likes then add a pinch of salt and the habesha butter for extra taste.
  8. Add the boiled eggs to the cooking pan and let them simmer with the cooked dish for 2 minutes and then take the cooking pan off the electric cooking stove and serve it on top of Injera laid on a plate.

3.Tsebhi Shiro

Top, Qulwa, Tsebhi Derho

  • Time: 45 minutes
  • Serves: 8 persons

Ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Chickpeas powder/shiro 1/2 kg
Onion 3 large pieces
Oil 1/2 cup
Garlic 1 head
Ground ginger 1/2 teaspoon
Black pepper 1/2 teaspoon
Water 3 cups
Salt 1 teaspoon

Preparation Steps

  1. Peel the onions and dice them in to small cubes (of 1/2 cm ). Peel and mash the garlic
  2. Heat the oil in a pan and add the onions and garlic. Sauté the onion and garlic until become golden color.
  3. Add theginger,pepper and salt and 2 cups of water.Stir well,cover the pan and bring it to boil.
  4. When the water boil, add the peas powder while stiring and then add the rest of the water(1 cup) and cover the pan and let it cook for 40 minutes on a medium-low flame.
  5. When it rightly cooked it gives like a volcano eruption sounds with a "tiq-toq"sounds and then turn off the flame/stove and serve the "shiro" while hot on top of Injera with green peppers on the side of the plate.
